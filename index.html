  • March 2020

    Our Humble Beginnings

    On March 7 we left on our foreign adventure but unfortunately this did not last long, after 10 days we had to return to Belgium. Our assignment was to check the security of smart home devices, such as what data they send back to their server and whether this is all done securely. Of course, due to the circumstances, we had to investigate everything at home with the devices that we had, so we started asking friends and family whether they had any smart devices. Fortunately, a friend still had some equipment and we could already start our internship.

  • March-April 2020

    Smart devices

    On the google devices we found relatively little information that could be harmful. Only on the google chromecast we found quite a lot of information about the device itself, such as which encryption she used and ip addresses and mac addresses. On the smart TV I researched, we could see which sites this person went to with the TV browser. Also a number of XML files where the serial number and other information about the TV itself were stored.

  • April-May 2020

    The paper

    At the end of April early May we started writing all our findings in a paper. Our supervisor Torben helped us to do all this in a correct template, we had never written a paper before so he helped us very well.

  • May 2020

    The end

    After finishing everything, I still come across a nice experience. We have been given the opportunity to work with a foreign client, which not every student can say about their internship.

Project 4.0

Hosting platform with AWS

In my last year I had the privilege to work with a team of six on a mobile application. The main goals of this project were three things: efficiency, thinking green and improving safety. The mobile application took care of tracking your location, giving you the fastest route and alarming one if he comes to close to a transportation vehicle. This would boost the efficiency of a docker in the port of Antwerp. I was mainly working on the hosting part of the app, we did this with AWS. We also needed a way to host the backend on our server and we did this with docker files.
